# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- **GitHub repositories** (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- **Discord server** and chat channels
- **Email communications** related to the project
- **Social media** when representing the project
- **Conferences and meetups** where the project is represented
- **Online or offline events** sponsored by the community

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

**Example Scenario**: Using mildly inappropriate language in a GitHub issue or discussion.

---

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

**Example Scenario**: Repeatedly making dismissive or hostile comments toward other contributors.

---

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

**Example Scenario**: Publishing someone's private information or engaging in harassment.

---

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

**Example Scenario**: Persistent harassment, threats, or repeated violations after warnings.

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it as soon as possible to:

- **Email**: conduct@neonexcore.dev
- **Discord**: Direct message to @Moderators
- **GitHub**: Flag the content and email moderators

### What to Include

When reporting an incident, please include:

1. **Your contact information** (so we can follow up)
2. **Names** (or usernames) of people involved
3. **Description** of the incident (what happened)
4. **Context** (when and where it occurred)
5. **Screenshots** or links (if available)
6. **Any additional information** that would be helpful

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality. We will not share your identity without your explicit consent, except where required by law.

### Response Time

You can expect:
- **Initial response**: Within 24 hours
- **Investigation**: Within 3-5 business days
- **Resolution**: As quickly as possible, depending on complexity

---

## Investigation Process

### Steps

1. **Acknowledgment**: Reporter receives confirmation within 24 hours
2. **Review**: Moderators review the report and gather additional information
3. **Decision**: Moderators determine appropriate action based on guidelines
4. **Communication**: All parties are informed of the decision
5. **Appeal**: Parties may appeal the decision within 7 days

### Principles

- **Fairness**: All parties will be heard
- **Transparency**: Decisions will be explained
- **Consistency**: Similar cases will be handled similarly
- **Privacy**: Personal information will be protected

## Examples of Positive Interactions

### Code Review

**Good Example:**
```
"Great work on this feature! I have a few suggestions:
1. Consider adding error handling for edge cases
2. The function name could be more descriptive

Happy to discuss these ideas further. Thanks for your contribution!"
```

**Bad Example:**
```
"This code is terrible. Did you even test this?"
```

### Discussion

**Good Example:**
```
"I understand your perspective, but I see this differently. 
Here's why I think approach A might be better than approach B...
What do you think?"
```

**Bad Example:**
```
"You're wrong. Anyone with basic knowledge would know this."
```

### Issue Reporting

**Good Example:**
```
"I found a bug in the user module. Here are the steps to reproduce:
1. Create a user with email X
2. Try to login
3. Error occurs

Environment: Go 1.21, PostgreSQL 15

Let me know if you need more information!"
```

**Bad Example:**
```
"This doesn't work. Fix it."
```

## Community Expectations

### For Contributors

- **Read the documentation** before asking questions
- **Search existing issues** before creating new ones
- **Provide context** when reporting bugs
- **Be patient** with response times
- **Accept feedback** gracefully
- **Test your changes** before submitting
- **Write clear commit messages**

### For Maintainers

- **Review PRs promptly** (within 48 hours)
- **Provide constructive feedback**
- **Explain decisions** clearly
- **Be welcoming** to newcomers
- **Recognize contributions**
- **Maintain consistent standards**
- **Lead by example**

### For Community Members

- **Welcome newcomers** warmly
- **Help others** when possible
- **Share knowledge** and experiences
- **Celebrate successes** together
- **Provide constructive feedback**
- **Respect different viewpoints**
- **Follow community guidelines**
